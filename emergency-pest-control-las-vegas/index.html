Scorpion Emergency · Step-by-Step Response

Scorpion Emergency: What to Do Right Now

If you find a bark scorpion inside your Las Vegas home, call Nevada Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222 if anyone is stung. Then call Pest Control Inc at (702) 228-4394. Do not lose sight of the scorpion's location.

A bark scorpion inside an occupied Las Vegas home is the single most common Tier 1 pest emergency in Clark County. The following six steps are the documented response, in order. The first step depends entirely on whether anyone has been stung.

  1. If Anyone Is Stung, Call Nevada Poison Control First

    Nevada Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222. They triage and direct medical response. Symptoms to monitor: severe pain at sting site, numbness or tingling, muscle twitching, difficulty swallowing, blurred vision, restlessness in young children. Most healthy adult bark scorpion stings do not require ER care, but Poison Control determines this for your specific situation. ER guidance when needed: Summerlin Hospital, Henderson Hospital, Sunrise Hospital, Valley View. Children under 5, elderly, and immunocompromised individuals have higher risk and may need ER triage.

  2. Do Not Lose Sight of the Scorpion

    Keep your eyes on its location. If safely possible, cover with a glass jar and slide cardboard underneath for safe containment until the technician arrives. A scorpion that escapes into a wall void becomes much harder to find, and may emerge somewhere worse later.

  3. Use UV Black Light to Check for Others

    Bark scorpions glow bright blue-green under 365nm UV light in complete darkness. A phone flashlight will not work, phone flashlights are white LED, not 365nm UV. Scan wall-floor junctions, baseboards, behind furniture, inside shoes and bags. One scorpion sighting often means more are present nearby.

  4. Isolate the Room if Possible

    Close the door and place a towel under the gap. Keep family members and pets out of the affected room until the technician arrives. Containment limits the search area when treatment begins.

  5. Do Not Use Consumer Spray

    Hardware-store scorpion spray drives scorpions deeper into wall voids, making professional treatment harder, not easier. The Health Conscious Service Program emergency response uses targeted methods that work with documented scorpion biology, not against it.

  6. Call Pest Control Inc

While You Wait · Do This / Don't Do This

While You Wait, What to Do Before the Technician Arrives

While waiting for emergency pest control: isolate the affected area, keep family and pets out, photograph the pest and its location, do not use consumer spray products, and have your address and pest description ready for the technician.

In the time between calling and the technician's arrival, the actions you take, and the actions you avoid, make a measurable difference in the speed and effectiveness of treatment. The following two lists are the documented Health Conscious Service Program "while you wait" protocol.

✓ Do This

✓
Isolate the affected areaClose the door of the affected room and place a towel under the gap. Exception: for confirmed bed bugs, stay in the room, do not migrate.
✓
Photograph everythingDated phone photos of the pest, its location, and any damage. Photos go into your written service record and help confirm species before arrival.
✓
Prepare the spaceClear access to baseboards and cabinet hinges. Move furniture a few inches from walls in the target area. Saves the technician time.
✓
Have information readyAddress, gate code, pest type, location, anyone stung, children, pets, chemical sensitivities. The dispatcher uses this for triage and the technician for treatment planning.
✓
Keep pets and children outUntil the technician communicates clearance. Containment makes treatment faster and safer.

✗ Don't Do This

✗
Use consumer spray productsHardware-store sprays scatter cockroach colonies into wall voids and drive scorpions deeper into structures, making professional treatment harder, not easier.
✗
Sleep in another room (bed bugs)The most counter-intuitive bed bug rule. Migrating to another room spreads the infestation to new areas. Stay put until the technician inspects.
✗
Attempt to remove a wasp nestColony defense response is dangerous. Africanized honey bees in Las Vegas Valley make this particularly risky. Wait for the technician.

Throw away the mattress before bed bug inspectionDestroys evidence the technician needs to confirm species and infestation level. Premature disposal also risks spreading bed bugs to other rooms or properties.
