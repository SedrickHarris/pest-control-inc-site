What You Need to Know About Pigeons in Las Vegas

Feral pigeons are the dominant pest bird in Las Vegas — a non-migratory species that lives and breeds year-round in the Las Vegas Valley’s warm, dry climate. Once pigeons establish a roosting or nesting site on a property they return reliably, and their numbers grow quickly. Pigeon droppings are highly acidic and corrosive, carrying diseases including histoplasmosis, cryptococcosis, and salmonella. Dry droppings become airborne dust — a respiratory risk for residents and employees. Solar panels, HVAC equipment, rooftop ledges, and building signs are the most common damage and roosting locations in Las Vegas. Feeding pigeons is illegal in the City of Las Vegas — $1,000 fine and up to 6 months. Effective pigeon control combines removal, exclusion, and droppings cleanup — deterrents alone without cleanup and exclusion do not produce lasting results.

WHY THIS MATTERS

Three Reasons Pigeons Are a Serious Problem in Las Vegas

Pigeon infestations are not a cosmetic nuisance. The damage they cause is structural, the health risk is real, and the financial cost compounds the longer a flock is allowed to establish.

Health Risk
🪥

Pigeon Droppings Carry Disease

Pigeon droppings carry over 60 diseases transmissible to humans including histoplasmosis (fungal lung disease from dried droppings), cryptococcosis, salmonella, and E. coli. Dry droppings become airborne particulate matter — a respiratory hazard for anyone with asthma, allergies, or compromised immune function. Pigeon nesting materials introduce mites, fleas, and ticks to structures. HVAC systems contaminated by pigeon roosting on or near intake vents can distribute pathogen-laden particulates throughout a building.

Property Damage
🏠

Droppings Are Acidic & Corrosive

Pigeon droppings are highly acidic — corrosive to roof membranes, paint, metal flashing, concrete, and solar panel surfaces. Accumulated droppings accelerate material degradation significantly. Nesting material clogs gutters and downspouts, causing water backup and roof damage. Pigeons roosting under solar panels cause wiring damage, reduce panel efficiency, and void manufacturer warranties on many systems. HVAC nest blockages reduce efficiency and create fire hazards.

Las Vegas Specific
🌞

Solar Panel Nesting Damage

Solar panel ownership is widespread in Las Vegas. Pigeons exploit the shaded gap under panels as ideal nesting and roosting sites — warm in winter, shaded in summer, elevated from predators. Nesting debris and droppings under panels reduce efficiency, damage wiring and mounting hardware, and in many cases void the manufacturer warranty. Solar panel exclusion mesh — a custom-fitted barrier around the panel perimeter — is the only permanent solution. Cleanup and re-exclusion after a pigeon infestation under panels is a common PCI service in Las Vegas.

DETERRENT & EXCLUSION METHODS

How Pigeons Are Removed and Kept Out

There is no single product that solves every pigeon problem. The right combination depends on the property, the roosting locations, and the size of the flock — and any deterrent installation must be preceded by full droppings and nesting material cleanup or the flock returns.

1

Bird Spikes

Stainless steel spikes installed on ledges, rooflines, gutters, and flat surfaces. Prevents landing and roosting. Effective for small surface areas and targeted deterrence. Not suitable for all locations — if installed incorrectly, pigeons build nests around and over the spikes.

Bird Netting

Durable mesh netting installed to completely exclude birds from specific areas — eaves, courtyards, HVAC equipment, and building overhangs. Most effective for large enclosed spaces. Used in both residential and commercial applications.

3

Solar Panel Exclusion Mesh

Custom-fitted steel mesh installed around the perimeter of solar panel arrays. Prevents access to the gap under panels entirely. Attached with UV-stable clips that do not scratch or damage panel frames. Black mesh is low-visibility from street level.

Solar arrays Warranty-safe install Permanent solution

Droppings Cleanup & Disinfection

Required before any deterrent installation — residual droppings and pheromones attract pigeons back to the same location. Cleanup removes droppings, nesting material, and sanitizes the area. For health and safety reasons, droppings cleanup should always be performed by professionals with appropriate protective equipment.

WHERE PIGEONS ROOST

Eight Locations Pigeons Roost and Nest on Las Vegas Properties

Las Vegas construction patterns — flat commercial rooftops, residential solar arrays, rooftop HVAC, and parapet walls — create predictable pigeon roosting sites year after year. These are the eight locations PCI technicians inspect first on every bird call.

1
Under Solar Panels

Most common damage location in residential Las Vegas. Shaded gap under panels is the preferred nesting site — cleanup plus exclusion mesh required.

2
Rooftop HVAC Equipment & Condensers

Warm in winter and protected from wind. Nest debris and droppings on or near intake vents create both fire and contamination risk.

3
Building Eaves & Soffits

Sheltered, shaded, and out of reach. Eave and soffit gaps allow pigeons access to attic voids and protected nesting cavities.

4
Ledges, Window Sills & Parapet Walls

Classic commercial pigeon roost. Targeted spike installation along ledges and parapets is the standard exclusion response.

5
Block Wall Tops & Fences

Used as flock staging and observation points between rooftop roosts. Often the first sign of an establishing local flock.

6
Patio Covers & Pergolas

Shaded structural framing of covered patios attracts both individual roosting and small nesting attempts. Spike or net retrofit recommended.

7
Signs & Commercial Building Facades

Backlit signs, facade ornamentation, and architectural recesses on commercial buildings are predictable pigeon nesting spots throughout the Las Vegas Valley.

8
Attic Voids via Roofline Gaps

Pigeons that find roofline gaps can establish inside the attic itself. Requires removal, cleanup, and structural sealing of the entry point.
