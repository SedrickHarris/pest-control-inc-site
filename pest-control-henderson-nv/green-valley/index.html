GREEN VALLEY PEST PRESSURE

Why Green Valley Homes Face Persistent Pest Pressure

Green Valley is the oldest master-planned community in Southern Nevada, and its age is the defining factor in its pest profile. Decades of mature palm trees, citrus trees, and ornamental landscaping provide ideal nesting sites and food sources for roof rats — which access homes through roof vents, utility penetrations, and overhanging branches. Roof rat pressure in Green Valley is among the highest in Henderson.

Older slab foundations throughout Green Valley predate modern sub-slab termite pre-treatment standards. Subterranean termites build mud tubes from soil to structural wood sources and are active year-round in Clark County soil — annual termite inspection is strongly recommended for any Green Valley home with a pre-2000 foundation. Black widows are common in block walls, garages, and outdoor storage areas throughout the community.

Argentine ants surge post-monsoon as colonies seek indoor moisture, following irrigation lines and foundation edges into homes. The Galleria at Sunset commercial corridor nearby adds German cockroach pressure in commercial-adjacent residential streets and multi-unit buildings.

The Health Conscious Service Program uses an exterior-first approach with EPA-registered low-impact baits and gels — well suited to the established landscaping and older construction common throughout Green Valley.
