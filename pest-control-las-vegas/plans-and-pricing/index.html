Pricing Factors

What Affects Pest Control Pricing

Every Las Vegas property is different. Pest Control Inc prices each plan based on actual conditions, not a flat rate.

Property Size and Structure Type

Square footage, number of stories, attached structures (garage, casita, pool house), and overall foot-trafficable perimeter all influence the service scope.

Pest Species and Infestation Severity

Different pests require different treatments and products. Active infestations require more intensive treatment than preventive service.

Service Frequency

One-time treatments, monthly recurring programs, and quarterly recurring programs are scoped differently based on the protection level needed.

Interior vs. Exterior-Only Treatment

Exterior-first treatment is the foundation of the Health Conscious Service Program. Interior treatment is included when conditions warrant.

Number of Structures on the Property

Detached garages, casitas, pool houses, sheds, and outbuildings each require coverage if pest pressure is present.

Geographic Location and Local Pest Pressure

Desert-edge properties, HOA communities, and neighborhoods with active scorpion or termite pressure may require different service profiles.

Frequency Options

Service Frequency Options

Three service frequencies for Las Vegas Valley homes and businesses. The free estimate process recommends the right one for your situation.

One-Time Treatment

Best For

Targeted problem requiring a single visit.

Includes

Inspection, species identification, targeted treatment, and follow-up if pests return (money-back guarantee applies).

Monthly Service

Best For

Homes with active scorpion, ant, or rodent pressure.

Includes

Exterior perimeter barrier refreshed every 30 days. Highest level of ongoing protection. Recommended for desert-edge properties and homes with active pest pressure.

Quarterly Service

Best For

General prevention for lower-pressure properties.

Includes

Interior and exterior treatment four times per year. Ideal for newer construction or properties with lower desert adjacency.
