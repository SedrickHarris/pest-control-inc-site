THE LAKES PEST PRESSURE

Why The Lakes Homes Face a Unique Pest Profile

The Lakes covers approximately two square miles of established west Las Vegas neighborhood built around a network of artificial lakes, trails, and HOA common areas. The persistent water features create moisture gradients that attract Argentine ants, earwigs, and cockroaches year-round, a different pest dynamic than drier desert-edge communities elsewhere in the valley.

Mature tree canopy throughout The Lakes provides roof rat harborage and travel corridors into attics and garages. Bark scorpions are present but typically at lower density than newer desert-edge developments, however they are still active in block walls, garages, and any rock or debris near the lake perimeter. Bird and pigeon pressure is elevated near water features and community common areas.

Subterranean termites are active in the soil surrounding homes built in the 1980s and 1990s, many of which predate modern sub-slab termite pre-treatment standards. Annual termite inspection is recommended for all older homes in The Lakes.

The Health Conscious Service Program uses an exterior-first approach with EPA-registered low-impact baits and gels, suited to the HOA-governed, water-adjacent environment of The Lakes where product selection and application method matter.
