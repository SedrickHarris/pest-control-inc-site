MOUNTAIN'S EDGE, LAS VEGAS NV 89148

A Maturing Southwest Las Vegas Master-Planned Community

Mountain's Edge is a large master-planned community in southwest Las Vegas, NV 89148. It was one of the fastest-growing communities in the valley during its build-out years, and today carries a mix of newer construction in the latest phases and now-maturing homes in the original sections. That age range is itself a pest-pressure factor, brand-new slabs and aging slabs each present different risk profiles for subterranean termites and roof rats.

The community's western and southern boundaries sit directly against undeveloped Mojave desert scrub. That desert interface is the single largest driver of bark scorpion and roof rat pressure: undisturbed land just beyond the perimeter wall supplies a continuous outdoor population that forages into the residential streets every night. Properties along the perimeter and those backing to open land see noticeably higher activity than interior streets.

As Mountain's Edge has aged, the landscaping has matured. Fruit trees, dense shrubs, and grown-in ornamental plantings around HOA common areas and individual homes create harborage and a food source for roof rats. Irrigation lines provide the water source. The result is a neighborhood where rodent and scorpion programs are the work of recurring service, not one-off treatment.

The Health Conscious Service Program uses EPA-registered low-impact baits and gels applied exterior-first, eco-friendly, family safe, and pet safe by design.

MOUNTAIN'S EDGE PEST PRESSURE

Top Pests in Mountain's Edge, NV

The pests that drive the most service calls in Mountain's Edge are tied directly to the community's desert scrub interface, maturing landscaping, and mix of newer and aging slab foundations. Each species below is treated under the Health Conscious Service Program.

Arizona Bark Scorpion

The primary concern along the western and southern edges of Mountain's Edge. Bark scorpions harbor in undisturbed desert soil and forage at night into the closest residential structures, block walls, rock landscaping, garages, and home perimeters. Perimeter streets and backing properties see the most activity.

Roof Rats

Roof rat pressure has grown alongside the community's landscaping. Fruit trees, dense vegetation, and mature canopy provide harborage and food, while irrigation supplies water. Desert adjacency on the western and southern edges keeps an outdoor population continuously moving into the residential perimeter.

Subterranean Termites

Active in Clark County soil year-round. Mountain's Edge homes span the full slab age range, from the most recently completed sections to original homes with now-aging slabs. Annual termite inspection is recommended for every property in the community.

Argentine & Pavement Ants

Argentine and pavement ants follow the irrigation lines that run through every Mountain's Edge HOA common area and individual yard. Shared planting zones and drip-irrigated landscaping give ant colonies a continuous water and food source. Ant calls peak in spring through early fall.

German & American Cockroaches

German and American cockroaches exploit moisture from irrigation systems and the density of attached and detached homes throughout master-planned blocks. Gel baiting under the Health Conscious Service Program targets harborage zones without high indoor chemical exposure.

Black Widow & Other Spiders

Block walls, garages, sheds, and rock landscaping throughout Mountain's Edge provide ideal harborage for black widow spiders. Exterior-first treatment combined with sweeping of harborage zones is the standard approach for desert-adjacent properties.
