How Pest Control Inc Commercial Programs Are Built, Inspection, Exclusion & Monitoring

Spray-and-leave pest control is not commercial pest control. A real commercial program is built on three mechanics: thorough inspection, physical exclusion and pest proofing, and ongoing monitoring with documented trend analysis. Treatment, when warranted, is the smallest component of the program, applied only where inspection has identified a need, using the lowest-impact method available. This is integrated pest management for businesses, and it is the methodology Pest Control Inc has used for three generations across every commercial industry we serve.

Commercial Pest Inspections & Audit

Every commercial program begins and continues with structured commercial pest inspections. Initial inspections produce a complete facility risk profile, entry points, harborage areas, sanitation gaps, structural vulnerabilities, and pest pressure assessment. Recurring inspections become the data layer of your audit-ready commercial pest program. We document every visit, every finding, and every recommendation in the format your auditors and property management records expect.

Commercial Pest Exclusion & Proofing

Commercial pest exclusion and pest proofing form the foundation of every long-term commercial program. Sealing entry points, repairing structural gaps, securing dock seals, addressing roof penetrations, and installing physical barriers eliminates the conditions pests need to enter and survive in your facility. Exclusion does what chemistry alone never can. It stops the problem at the source.

Perimeter, Interior & Exterior Programs

Every commercial property gets a layered approach: commercial perimeter pest control to stop pests before they reach the building, commercial exterior pest control around the structure itself, and commercial interior pest control for any pressure that breaks through. The three layers work together, and the program documentation shows you exactly what was done at each layer on every visit.

Rodent Monitoring & Documented Programs

Commercial rodent control is rarely about a single treatment. It is about a documented monitoring program. Exterior bait stations, interior monitoring devices, sanitation reviews, and exclusion repair work together as a rodent compliance program. Every station, every check, and every finding is logged for your records and your auditors. This is what auditable rodent control looks like.
