Critical commercial risk · health hazard · reputation damage

Inventory contamination · product recall risk · supply-chain compliance risk

Stored-product pests arrive in incoming shipments and spread rapidly through dry goods inventory. Wasps & Bees

Stinging injury liability · OSHA recordable incident · outdoor area closure

Spiders & Black Widows

Customer and employee safety liability · outdoor area disruption

Black widows are common in Las Vegas commercial properties, particularly in outdoor storage areas, parking structures, and landscaping. Pigeons

Pigeon droppings contain uric acid that degrades building materials and HVAC systems. Accumulated droppings near building entries create slip-and-fall liability. Histoplasmosis, a fungal respiratory disease, is associated with pigeon roost cleanup in enclosed spaces.

Silverfish damage paper documents, book archives, fabric inventory, and wallpaper. For document-intensive businesses, legal offices, medical records, archival facilities, silverfish damage is a data integrity concern. For clothing and fabric retailers, fabric pests cause direct merchandise loss.

PEST WARNING SIGNS · COMMERCIAL PROPERTY · LAS VEGAS

How to Tell If Your Las Vegas Business Has a Pest Problem

Most commercial pest infestations are detectable before they become crises, if you know what to look for. These are the early warning signs that business owners and facility managers should inspect for monthly.

1

Receiving & Loading Area

Check for: Rodent droppings along wall bases and corners, gnaw marks on cardboard and packaging, rodent entry points at dock door gaps and utility penetrations, cockroach activity in cardboard accumulations, stored-product pest larvae or moths in dry goods deliveries.

2

Storage & Inventory Areas

Check for: Cockroach fecal spots or cast skins in cabinet corners and under equipment, rodent droppings near storage shelving, fly clusters near drains or waste areas, drain fly emergence from floor drains, ant trails to break-room or supply areas, unusual musty odors indicating pest activity.

3

Break Rooms & Shared Spaces

Check for: Cockroach activity in appliance motor housings and cabinet hinges, rodent droppings near appliances and under sinks, ant trails to break-room beverage and supply areas, fly activity near waste receptacles, evidence of pantry pest activity in dry-goods storage.

4

Common Areas, Lobby & Customer-Facing Spaces

Check for: Spider webs and black widow egg sacs at corners and lighting fixtures, ant activity near entry points and vending areas, fly activity at building entries, wasp nest development at eaves and exterior lighting, any evidence of rodent activity (droppings, gnaw marks, tracks).

5

Exterior Perimeter & Parking

Check for: Rodent burrow activity at landscape perimeter, scorpion harborage in rock landscaping and monument bases (Las Vegas-specific), wasp nest development at eaves and utility housings, pigeon roosting and accumulated droppings, ant mound activity adjacent to building foundation.

6

Trash & Waste Areas

Check for: Rodent activity at dumpster surrounds and enclosure walls, fly breeding in dumpster overflow, cockroach activity in dumpster enclosure interior, ant foraging from waste areas into building. Trash areas are the highest-density pest pressure zone in most commercial properties, inspect weekly.
