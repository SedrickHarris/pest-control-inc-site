BED BUG CONTROL · LAS VEGAS, NV

What You Need to Know About Bed Bugs in Las Vegas

Bed bugs are one of the most difficult pest infestations to eliminate in Las Vegas. The Las Vegas Valley’s tourism economy, with millions of hotel guests annually, creates constant bed bug introduction pressure into residential homes and apartments. Bed bugs do not fly or jump; they hitchhike on luggage, clothing, used furniture, and bedding. A single mated female introduced into a home can establish a full infestation within weeks. DIY treatments and over-the-counter sprays consistently fail because they do not penetrate harborage sites or eliminate eggs. Professional heat treatment or targeted chemical treatment is required for complete elimination.

WHY LAS VEGAS IS A HOT ZONE

Why Las Vegas Has a Bed Bug Problem

Three structural conditions make Las Vegas one of the most active bed bug markets in the United States. Understanding the introduction routes is the first step in preventing reinfestation after treatment.

🏨
Tourism Economy

Las Vegas hosts tens of millions of hotel and resort guests annually. Hotel bed bug pressure is among the highest of any US city. Guests who stay in infested rooms can unknowingly carry bed bugs home in luggage and clothing. This creates a constant reintroduction cycle that makes Las Vegas one of the most active bed bug markets in the country.

🏘
Multi-Unit Housing

Apartments, condos, and multi-unit properties allow bed bugs to spread between units through wall voids, electrical conduits, and shared spaces. A single infested unit in a complex can spread to neighboring units within weeks. Multi-unit property managers should treat adjacent units simultaneously.

🛒
Used Furniture & Online Marketplaces

A significant percentage of Las Vegas bed bug introductions come from used furniture, mattresses, sofas, and bed frames acquired through online marketplaces, garage sales, and thrift stores. Bed bugs can survive for months without feeding, making used furniture a reliable transmission vector even when no infestation signs are visible.

IDENTIFICATION

What Bed Bugs Look Like, And How to Spot Them

Bites alone are not a reliable indicator of bed bugs, many other pests cause similar bite marks. Confirmed identification requires finding the bugs, their eggs, or their fecal staining in harborage sites.

Appearance
What bed bugs look like

Adults: Small (1 to 7mm), flat, oval, reddish-brown. Swell and darken to deeper reddish-brown after feeding.

Eggs: White, approximately 1mm, glued to surfaces in tight seams and crevices.

Nymphs: Smaller than adults and translucent. Difficult to spot without knowing where to look.

Adult bed bugs are roughly the size of an apple seed and are visible to the naked eye, but they hide in tight harborage sites during the day and feed at night.

SIGNS OF INFESTATION

Six signs your home has bed bugs

  1. Small reddish-brown bugs visible on mattress seams, box spring, and headboard
  2. Dark rust-colored fecal stains on mattress, sheets, and wall near bed
  3. Tiny white eggs or shed skins (casings) in seams and crevices
  4. Sweet, musty odor in heavily infested rooms
  5. Bite marks, typically in lines or clusters on exposed skin, though bites alone cannot confirm bed bugs (other pests also bite)
  6. Blood stains on sheets from crushed bugs during sleep
TREATMENT OPTIONS

Two Professional Treatment Methods That Actually Work

Bed bug treatment failures almost always trace back to incomplete reach into harborage sites or failure to kill eggs. The two professional methods below both eliminate all life stages, they just take different paths to get there.

METHOD 1 · HEAT
Heat Treatment

Raises room temperature to 120 to 140°F for several hours. Penetrates all harborage sites, mattress seams, wall voids, baseboards, furniture. Kills all life stages including eggs in a single treatment. No chemical residue.

Requires preparation and being out of the home during treatment. Most effective for severe or widespread infestations.

Single Session No Residue Kills Eggs Out During Treatment
METHOD 2 · CHEMICAL
Chemical Treatment

Targeted application of EPA-registered residual products to harborage sites, mattress seams, box spring, baseboards, furniture joints, electrical outlets. Multiple visits required to catch nymphs that hatch after initial treatment.

Less disruptive than heat treatment. Most effective for early-stage or contained infestations. Do not wash or clean treated surfaces between visits.

2 to 3 Visits Targeted Application Less Disruption Best for Early-Stage
